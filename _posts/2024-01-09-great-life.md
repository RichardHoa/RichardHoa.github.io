Recently, I had a conversation with a team and we talked about how to have a good life, everyone's opinions are interesting, some say you need to have high morale, and some say you need continuous learning, I agree with them all. Though the conversation has passed, my mind still lingers with that question: "What makes a good life?", this blog post investigates the question further.

Before going to the solution, let's define what "great" means in this context. You have your answers (or maybe not 🤨) and I have mine. With me, a "great" life means I have a lot of experiences. It's that simple. Of course, I want positive experiences more than negative experiences. But negative or positive is just the labeling process we put into an experience to describe it. An experience, at its core, is just an experience. It's neither negative nor positive. Having a bad grade, experiencing a family loss, making more money, getting a good grade, getting long-wanted possessions. All of these are experiences. Let's imagine each positive experience is a bright pixel and each negative experience is a dark pixel, all of the pixels combined together to create a picture, a picture of your life. 

So, what kind of picture you would like to have?

### In essence, I want tons of experiences.

Move on to the solution part, how can you get more of any kind of experiences in your life?

This depends on your life, and it won't be that hard to figure it out for yourself. For example, trying a new route to home is an experience. Maybe try new food. How about going to the museum next week? What about scheduling an informal talk to find out more about your colleagues? Google is your friend, there are literally tons of suggestions out there, and tons of new activities for you to do.

Remember, not all new experiences are going to be positive. Maybe it's a disaster going to the museum and you don't enjoy it even a bit. It's okay, those dark pixels (negative experiences) will make your life memorable. The keyword here is **experience**, it's not about looking for only good
