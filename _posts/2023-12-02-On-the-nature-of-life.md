I just had the first counselling session ever in my entire life. And it's amazing. It got me thinking about the nature of life. It's always a mix of good and bad. I don't want to use extreme words like "always" or "never," but I really think there's always good and bad. Social media nowadays only presents the good, doesn't it? It's about being successful, being disciplined, being in the top 1%, and doing something spectacular. These are certainly good things; we should live to the best of our capacity. I truly believe in it. But sometimes it can backfire, like the feeling of being worthless when you're not being productive. With the obsession that your life has to be 100% optimised, there's no "waste" time whatsoever. You've got to work towards creating something valuable all the time.

I don't think life is that perfect; I don't think you can be productive 100% of the time at all. Sometimes, you have a "guilty" pleasure, and you just need to accept it. It's not "guilty" pleasure; it's pleasure. Accept that your life is not optimised 100%, that your life is not perfect, and that you're not productive all the time. And your value is not determined by your productivity.

It's a huge struggle for me to accept that I'll have unproductive time. For all of my existence, I've been trying to fight being unproductive again. It's like wrestling; one side is me, and the other side is a YouTube video or social media post. I always discipline myself to focus, but what if I just leave the game? I just stopped wrestling. Whenever the desire comes, I acknowledge that there is a desire to watch Facebook posts. Sometimes I'll act on it, sometimes I won't. But I don't have to discipline myself anymore. It's just about acceptance.

You do it anyway, so instead of feeling guilty about what you always do, why not just accept it as a part of your life and go on? Accept that you watch Facebook reels and all the lingering effects they have on your focus later. Accept it as a part of your life.

Now, if you spend most of your day watching Facebook and it negatively impacts your life, that's an addiction if you wish. You need to find a solution, but if you scroll through Facebook and get all of your tasks done, your life is normal, no relationship is destroyed, and no fun is missed. That's super fine; everyone has something to entertain themselves with, whether it's social media or games or whatever, or maybe a mix of all those things. It's just normal. What's the point of feeling bad about something that you do daily? It just adds more negativity to your day, nothing else. That negativity doesn't help you become a better person.
