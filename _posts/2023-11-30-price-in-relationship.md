I've been in a relationship for roughly nine months; it's a big time for a child like me. One thing I see is that relationships are always a mix of both ups and downs, and that's maybe the nature of life. 

There are happy moments when the two come together and have an amazing time, but there are also tough moments when you want to break up so badly that you don't have to let your ego down. That's the cost of being in a relationship. 

Leaving your ego behind and growing up is extremely painful; it's not easy; it's not like reading a spiritual book and suddenly your ego vanishes away. It's a tough, tough process. It's just that you have to pay the price, I don't see any other way around. If you find a shortcut, please let me know. If you don't want to pay the price and go through tough moments, sure, you can always break up and have amazing alone time. 

You always have options. But you won't experience those joyful moments that only exist in romantic relationships.

Sooooo, what's the lesson here? 

Expect painful moments; I guarantee with you they'll come and may come a lot. When you're in those moments, remind yourself that you're just being a good universal citizen and pay the price for all those good memory. It's a price to pay.
